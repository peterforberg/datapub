Ingredients

  • 5 pounds chicken thigh (with or without skin)
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 3 jalapenos, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, squashed and minced
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Add your olive oil to a large, heavy bottomed pot or dutch oven. Over a medium-high heat, brown your lightly salted chicken thighs. Combine your garlic, soy sauce, vinegar,water, and brown sugar in a separate bowl and pour them over your chicken. Add in your peppers. Cover and bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. This would be a good time to start on your white rice. Let the chicken simmer until fully cooked, about half an hour. If you want a thicker sauce at the end, remove the chicken from the pot and stir in a small slurry of cornstarch, then let simmer for 5-10 minutes. Serve chicken with sauce over rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 5 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves of garlic, squashed and minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger (optional)
  • a splash of orange juice
  • a splash of sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 2 pounds of salmon
  • 3 heads of bok choy
  • 1/2 tablespoon olive oil

Look, sometimes, when I am feeling lazy, I just buy the teriyaki marinade from Aldi, and sometimes, if I can be really honest, I think it's better. Anyways! Combine your water, brown sugar, soy sauce, honey, one clove of garlic, ginger, orange juice, and sesame oil in a bowl and mix well. Start simmering this sauce on a medium heat. Then make a small slurry of your cornstarch and mix it in. Let simmer to desired thickness and then let cool on a different burner.

Once cooled, add the majority of the sauce to a plastic bag with the salmon. Marinate for at least 20 minutes. When you're close to done marinating, start the broiler (or preheat your oven to 425) and then bake the salmon 12-16 minutes or until cooked. While the salmon cooks, break apart your bok choy and heat up the remainder of your garlic and olive oil in a frying pan. Add your bok choy and cook until softened, about 4 minutes. Remove the salmon from the oven, serve with bok choy, the remaining teriyaki marinade, and sesame seeds.
